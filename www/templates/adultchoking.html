Adult Choking (Airway Obstruction)

An airway obstruction means that something is blocking a person’s airway, which prevents air from moving in and out of the lungs. A blockage can partially or completely obstruct the airflow, and this can occur either when the person is conscious or unconscious. An airway obstruction is referred to as “choking”.

Signs and Symptoms of Choking

• Visibly distressed

• Suddenly unable to speak

• Unable to cough

• Unable to breathe

• Wheezing or gasping

• Casualty may grasp their throat which is the universal sign for choking

First Aid for Adult Choking

1. Ask "Are you choking?"

Call for help! Get someone to call 911!


2. Clear the obstruction

A.

Give five back blows

B.

Give five abdominal thrusts

Option for pregnant:

A.

Give five back blows

B.

Give five chest thrusts


Other Options:

A.

Seated chest thrusts

B.

Seated chest thrusts

C.

Self-administered thrusts


3. Once the obstruction is cleared, place the casualty in a comfortable position and monitor breathing until EMS is on the scene. Conscious casualty whose airway has been cleared should be assessed by a physician due to a risk of infection and swelling of the airway.
