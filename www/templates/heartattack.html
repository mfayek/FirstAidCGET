When the delivery of oxygen to the heart muscle is decreased over a long period of time, the damage to the heart muscle often becomes irreversible. The affected heart muscle cells eventually die and the injured area of the heart cannot pump adequately.

Signs and Symptoms of Angina and Heart Attack

• Tightness or squeezing sensation in the chest behind the breastbone:

• Crushing, vice-like pressure in the chest

• Nausea, heartburn and indigestion

• General feeling of fatigue and weakness

• Sore, aching jaw or arm

• Fear and anxiety

• Pale, cool and sweaty skin

• Denial

• Shortness of breath

• Fainting

Signs and Symptoms of Angina and Heart Attack can be different for women

Up to 43% of women report no chest pain and were likely to experience vague symptoms such as shortness of breath, unusual tiredness or fatigue, and sleep disturbances.

• The heart attack symptoms women report are:

• Pain or discomfort in the back, usually along the bra line

• Sudden weakness, fatigue, or dizziness

• Swelling of the feet, ankles, and lower legs

• Nausea and vomiting

• Stomach or abdominal pain

• Anxiety

• Very fast pulse

• Pain in the lower jaw

• Clammy skin

First Aid for Heart Attack

1 Assess level of responsiveness


2 Call 911


3 Assess the ABC's


4 Ask the person to sit in a comfortable position


5 Give the person his or her medication such as two chewable 81mg ASA (Aspirin®) or nitroglycerin medication (1)

6 Treat for shock


Ask the following questions:


• Where is the pain located? Severity of the pain (1 to 10) ?


• Last meal (indigestion)? Past medical history?


Warning (1):

1. Do not give ASA (acetylsalicylic acid), to a person with the following conditions: allergy to Aspirin®, asthma, ulcers, head injury and CVA. Follow medical directions from a Health Care Professional on the use of ASA and Nitroglycerin.

2. Do not give nitroglycerin to a person who has taken erectile dysfunction medication within the last 48hrs.
