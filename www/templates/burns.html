Burns result from the direct contact of heat on the skin and the underlying surfaces. They are generally classified in terms of depth and according to the layer of skin that has been affected.

Burn Classification

First Degree :

(Superficial) involves the epidermis or top layer

Second degree :

(Partial Thickness) involves dermis or middle layer

Third degree :

(Full Thickness) involves the subcutaneous layer or bottom layer

• To stop burning: remove any clothing or jewellery from the burn site if possible


• If clothing is stuck to the skin; don’t tear it off, cut around it.


• Sterile dressings and flushing the wound with water.


• Use water based gel for burns (if available)


• Keep the person warm and treat for shock

Chemical Burns

• Remove the person from the chemical spill area


• Brush off the powder chemical


• Sterile dressings and flushing the wound with water.


• Rince off the liquid chemical


• Treat for shock
