There are various causes of CVA although they are generally attributed to a rupture (from an aneurysm) or an occlusion (blood clot) in a cerebral blood vessel. As with heart muscle cells, when blood flow to the brain is compromised, brain cells are also damaged and this causes alterations in brain function. Strokes result from a reduction or stoppage of blood flow and oxygen supply to a part of the brain.

Signs and Symptoms of a Stroke (CVA)

FAST Check:


• Facial droop

• Arm drift

• Speech impairment

• Time to get help immediately.

First Aid for Stroke

• Check for level of responsiveness before calling 911


• Assess the ABC's


• Treat for shock


Ongoing Casualty Care:


Ask the person to sit or lie in a comfortable position.


Ask the person to smile and to squeeze both your hands.


Also ask them to touch their nose with one finger of each hand, one at a time to assess for lack of coordination.
