First Aid for CPR

1 Check level of responsiveness

2 Call for Help, call 911 and get the AED

3 Open the airway and check for no breathing or no normal breathing

4 If no breathing or no normal breathing; Perform CPR for about 2 minutes

5 30 chest compressions : 2 breaths (5 cycles)

6 Turn AED power ON

7 Select and attach AED electrodes

8 Wait for AED instructions (shock or no shock advised)

9 If shock advised, press the shock button

10 Follow AED instructions

The rescuer must ensure that the path of energy delivered does not flow from the defibrillator to the operator.


Push Hard and Push Fast

Deeper compressions increase blood flow to the brain and vital organs such as the heart, lungs, kidneys and liver. It also decreases the risk of blood coagulation (clotting) and extend heart fibrillation.
